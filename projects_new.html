Resilience in Low Power and Lossy Internet of Things Networks

Sponsor: IIT (ISM) Dhanbad, India.

Scheme: Faculty Research Scheme

Duration: October 2024 - October 2026

Cost: 14.89 Lacs

Role: Principal Investigator

Project Objectives

The project aims to improve cell scheduling techniques in 6TiSCH networks by developing energy-efficient methods that enhance network resilience, especially in low-power and lossy networks. One of this project's objectives is to ensure efficient cell scheduling, which is crucial in IoT networks to maintain reliable communication while minimizing energy consumption.

Additionally, the project addresses improvements to the RPL (Routing Protocol for Low-Power and Lossy Networks) by designing parent selection algorithms considering network congestion, node energy levels, and current load. This ensures more stable routing paths and reduces the likelihood of network bottlenecks, enhancing overall network performance. Security is also a central focus, with efforts to detect and mitigate RPL attacks through lightweight Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and countermeasures. By implementing protocols on a testbed, the project also aims to generate a dataset reflecting real-world conditions, enabling AI/ML-based solutions for attack detection and contributing to more resilient IoT networks.
